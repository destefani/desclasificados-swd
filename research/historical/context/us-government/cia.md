# Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)

**Role**: Principal foreign intelligence and counterintelligence agency of the US government
**Founded**: 1947 (from OSS)
**Relevance**: Primary agency conducting covert operations in Chile

---

## Organizational Structure (1970s)

### Four Main Directorates

| Directorate | Function | Relevance to Chile |
|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Directorate of Operations (DO)** | Clandestine human intelligence (HUMINT), covert action | Primary operator of Chile programs |
| **Directorate of Intelligence (DI)** | Analysis of intelligence | Produced assessments on Chile |
| **Directorate of Science & Technology (DS&T)** | Technical collection | Supporting role |
| **Directorate of Administration (DA)** | Logistics, support | Supporting role |

### Name Change

In 1973, DCI James Schlesinger renamed the **Directorate of Plans (DDP)** to **Directorate of Operations (DO)**. Documents before 1973 may reference "DDP" instead of "DO".

---

## Directorate of Operations

The DO (formerly DDP) was responsible for Chile operations.

### Key Components

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Western Hemisphere Division** | Oversaw Latin America operations |
| **Counterintelligence Staff** | Vetted assets, offensive counterespionage |
| **Covert Action Staff** | Propaganda, political action, paramilitary |

### Field Structure

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Station** | CIA office within an embassy, officers under diplomatic cover |
| **Base** | Larger facility, often with airfield and training areas |
| **Chief of Station (COS)** | Senior CIA officer at a station |
| **Case Officer** | CIA officer handling agents/assets |
| **Asset** | Foreign individual recruited to provide intelligence |

---

## CIA Chile Station

Located within the US Embassy in Santiago.

### Key Functions (1960s-1970s)

1. **Intelligence collection** on Chilean politics
2. **Political action** - funding parties, media
3. **Covert action** - destabilization operations
4. **Liaison** with Chilean security services

### Allied Support

At CIA's request, the Australian Secret Intelligence Service (ASIS) established a station in Santiago in 1971 to support US operations. When Australia closed its station, it returned management of Chilean informants to the CIA.

## Chain of Command for Covert Action

```
President
    ↓
National Security Advisor (Kissinger)
    ↓
40 Committee (authorization)
    ↓
Director of Central Intelligence (DCI)
    ↓
Deputy Director for Operations (DDO)
    ↓
Western Hemisphere Division Chief
    ↓
Chile Station Chief
    ↓
Case Officers
    ↓
Assets/Agents
```

### Track II Exception

For Track II (military coup plotting), the chain bypassed normal oversight:

```
President Nixon
    ↓
National Security Advisor (Kissinger)
    ↓
DCI (Helms)
    ↓
DDO (Karamessines)
    ↓
Chile Station
```

State Department and Defense Department were excluded.

## CIA Operations in Chile

### Funding Summary (1963-1973)

| Purpose | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| Political parties | ~$4 million |
| Media operations | ~$1.5 million |
| Other covert action | ~$2.5 million |
| **Total (1970-1973 alone)** | **$8 million** |

### Major Programs

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Support to PDC** | Funding Christian Democrats against Allende |
| **El Mercurio** | $1.6+ million to opposition newspaper |
| **Opposition parties** | Funding to prevent/destabilize Allende |
| **Track II** | Military coup plotting (1970) |
| **Economic destabilization** | "Make the economy scream" |

## Communication Patterns

### Station to Headquarters

- **Cables**: Routine reporting, requests for guidance
- **Operational cables**: Asset reports, meeting summaries
- **Intelligence reports**: Finished analysis from field

### Headquarters to Station

- **Guidance cables**: Policy direction
- **Authorization cables**: Approval for operations
- **Intelligence requirements**: What to collect

### Code Names

| Code Name | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **FUBELT** | Chile Track II operation |
| **[STATION]** | CIA terminology (actual station names classified) |

---

## CIA-DINA Relationship

After the 1973 coup, CIA maintained contact with DINA (Pinochet's secret police):

- Intelligence liaison relationship
- Information sharing
- Awareness of DINA activities

The extent of CIA knowledge of DINA operations (including Operation Condor) remains partially classified.

---

## Church Committee Findings on CIA-Chile

1. CIA conducted **$8 million** in covert operations against Allende (1970-1973)
2. Track II was conducted **without 40 Committee oversight**
3. CIA was in contact with **coup plotters** before September 1973
4. CIA support to media played role in **creating conditions** for coup
5. Only **Chile case study** was publicly released (other 6 remained classified)

---

## Relevance to Document Corpus

CIA documents in the corpus typically include:
- **Station cables** (Santiago to Langley)
- **Headquarters cables** (Langley to Santiago)
- **Intelligence assessments** on Chilean politics
- **Memoranda** on covert action planning
- **40 Committee briefing materials**

Look for:
- References to "Station" or "Headquarters"
- Code names for operations
- Distribution to DDO, DCI, Western Hemisphere Division

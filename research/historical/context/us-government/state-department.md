## Communication Types

### Cable/Telegram

Primary means of rapid communication.

| From | To | Common Subjects |
|------|----|-----------------|
| SECSTATE | AMEMBASSY SANTIAGO | Policy guidance, instructions |
| AMEMBASSY SANTIAGO | SECSTATE | Political reporting, requests |

### Airgram

Longer reports sent via diplomatic pouch:
- Detailed political analyses
- Economic reports
- Biographical information on foreign officials
- Less time-sensitive materials

### Memoranda

Internal documents:
- Memoranda of Conversation (meeting records)
- Action Memoranda (request decisions)
- Information Memoranda (background)

## State Department vs CIA

### Institutional Tension

| Issue | State Department | CIA |
|-------|------------------|-----|
| **Track II** | Excluded | Conducted operation |
| **Ambassador's role** | Chief of Mission authority | Station chief reported to Langley |
| **Human rights** | Often advocated concern | Operational focus |
| **Transparency** | Diplomatic reporting | Covert action |

### The "Station vs Embassy" Dynamic

CIA stations operate within embassies but:
- Report through CIA chain (not Ambassador)
- May conduct operations Ambassador doesn't know about
- Have separate communications channels

Ambassador Korry famously did not know about Track II operations being conducted from "his" embassy.

## State Department and Chile Policy

### Pre-Coup (1970-1973)

- **Official policy**: Work with elected Allende government
- **Reality**: Track I operations coordinated with State; Track II excluded State
- **Tension**: Career diplomats vs. Nixon/Kissinger policy

### Post-Coup (1973-1990)

- Initial support for junta
- Growing human rights concerns (especially under Carter)
- Letelier investigation created diplomatic crisis
- Gradual pressure for democratic transition

## Document Reading Tips

### Identifying State Department Documents

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **SECSTATE** | From Secretary of State |
| **AMEMBASSY** | From/to American Embassy |
| **ARA** | Inter-American Affairs Bureau |
| **INR** | Intelligence and Research |
| **S/S** | Executive Secretariat |
| **P** | Policy Planning Staff |

### Distribution Lists

- **ACTION** copy: Must respond/act
- **INFO** copy: For information only
- Wide distribution suggests routine matter
- Limited distribution suggests sensitivity

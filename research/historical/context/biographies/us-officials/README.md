# US Officials Biographies

Biographical profiles of key US officials involved in Chile policy (1964-1990).

---

## Henry Kissinger (1923-2023)

**Positions**:
- National Security Advisor (1969-1975)
- Secretary of State (1973-1977)
- Chairman, 40 Committee

**Significance**: The most powerful figure in US Chile policy. Kissinger directed both Track I and Track II operations against Allende, chaired the 40 Committee that authorized covert action, and later served as Secretary of State during the coup and early Pinochet period.

**Key Actions**:
- June 1970: "I don't see why we need to stand by and watch a country go communist due to the irresponsibility of its own people"
- September 1970: Directed Track II military coup plotting
- September 1973: Told Nixon after coup "We didn't do it. I mean we helped them... created the conditions"
- Later misled President Ford about extent of US involvement

**Corpus mentions**: 24 documents

---

## Richard Nixon (1913-1994)

**Position**: President (1969-1974)

**Significance**: Ordered covert action against Allende. On September 15, 1970, gave DCI Helms explicit instructions to prevent Allende from taking power.

**Key Actions**:
- September 15, 1970: "Make the economy scream"
- Authorized $10 million for Chile operations
- Ordered Track II without 40 Committee approval

---

## Richard Helms (1913-2002)

**Position**: Director of Central Intelligence (1966-1973)

**Significance**: Received Nixon's Track II order directly. Managed CIA operations against Allende.

**Key Actions**:
- Took notes at September 15, 1970 meeting with Nixon
- Oversaw Track I and Track II operations
- Later testified to Church Committee

**Famous notes from Nixon meeting**:
> "1 in 10 chance perhaps, but save Chile!... make the economy scream"

---

## Edward Korry (1922-2003)

**Position**: US Ambassador to Chile (1967-1971)

**Significance**: Ambassador during Allende's election, deliberately excluded from Track II. Later fought to clear his name.

**Background**: Former journalist (UP, Look Magazine). Ambassador to Ethiopia (1963-1967) before Chile. Considered "probably the best Ambassador we have in Latin America."

**Key Points**:
- Opposed military coup option
- Was NOT informed of Track II operations being run from his embassy
- Publicly blamed for coup involvement for years
- Vindicated in 1981 when NYT acknowledged he knew nothing of CIA coup plotting

**Corpus mentions**: 36 documents (57 including variants)

---

## Thomas Karamessines (1917-1978)

**Position**: Deputy Director for Operations/Plans, CIA (1967-1973)

**Significance**: Managed Track II operations directly under Kissinger's guidance.

**Key Actions**:
- Direct supervisor of Chile operations at CIA
- Regular contact with Kissinger on Track II
- Testified to Church Committee

---

## William Colby (1920-1996)

**Position**: Director of Central Intelligence (1973-1976)

**Significance**: DCI during Church Committee investigations. Provided extensive testimony about Chile operations.

**Key Actions**:
- Disclosed CIA "Family Jewels" to Congress
- Testified about Chile covert actions
- Cooperated with Church Committee

---

## Nathaniel Davis (1925-2011)

**Position**: US Ambassador to Chile (1971-1973)

**Significance**: Ambassador during Allende government and the coup. Present in Santiago on September 11, 1973.

**Key Points**:
- Served throughout Allende's presidency
- Reported on deteriorating situation
- In country during coup

---

## George H.W. Bush (1924-2018)

**Position**: Director of Central Intelligence (1976-1977)

**Significance**: DCI during post-Watergate reforms and Letelier investigation period.

---

## Jack Kubisch

**Position**: Assistant Secretary of State for Inter-American Affairs (1973-1974)

**Significance**: Key State Department official immediately after coup.

**Key moment**: Day after coup, discussed with colleagues "how much advance notice we had on the coup" and "doubted if Dr. Kissinger would use this information, for it would reveal our close contact with coup leaders."

---

## Cyrus Vance (1917-2002)

**Position**: Secretary of State (1977-1980)

**Significance**: Carter administration's human rights focus created new tensions with Pinochet regime.

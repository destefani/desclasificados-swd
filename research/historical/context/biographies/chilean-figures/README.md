# Chilean Figures Biographies

Biographical profiles of key Chilean figures appearing in the declassified documents.

---

## Salvador Allende (1908-1973)

**Position**: President of Chile (1970-1973)
**Party**: Socialist Party (PS)
**Corpus mentions**: 133 documents (most mentioned person)

### Background

- Co-founder of Chilean Socialist Party (1933)
- Senator, Cabinet Minister under multiple governments
- Presidential candidate: 1952, 1958, 1964, 1970
- First democratically elected Marxist president in Latin America

### Key Events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| September 4, 1970 | Elected President (36.3% plurality) |
| November 3, 1970 | Inaugurated |
| 1971 | Nationalized copper industry |
| 1971-1973 | Implemented "Chilean road to socialism" |
| September 11, 1973 | Died during coup (officially suicide) |

### US Response

- Nixon ordered destabilization: "Make the economy scream"
- $8 million in covert action against his government
- Track II aimed to prevent his inauguration

## Orlando Letelier (1932-1976)

**Position**: Ambassador, Foreign Minister, Defense Minister under Allende
**Corpus mentions**: 102 documents (129 including variants)

### Background

- Career diplomat
- Chilean Ambassador to United States (1971-1973)
- Foreign Minister, then Defense Minister under Allende
- Detained after coup, held at Dawson Island

### Assassination

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| September 21, 1976 | **Assassinated** by DINA car bomb in Washington DC |
| | Ronni Moffitt also killed |
| | Michael Townley built the bomb |

### Significance

- Only foreign government assassination in US capital
- FBI investigation exposed DINA, led to its dissolution
- Major diplomatic crisis between US and Chile
- **132 documents** mention "Letelier assassination" as keyword

## Augusto Pinochet (1915-2006)

**Position**: Army Commander, Dictator (1973-1990)
**Corpus mentions**: 45 documents

### Background

- Professional Army officer
- Rose through ranks without political involvement
- Appointed Army Commander by Allende (August 23, 1973)
- Informed of coup plans only 3 days before (September 8)

### Key Events

| Period | Role |
|--------|------|
| 1973-1974 | President of Military Junta |
| 1974-1990 | "Supreme Chief of the Nation" / President |
| 1990-1998 | Army Commander-in-Chief |
| 1998-2000 | Arrested in London |
| 2006 | Died without conviction |

### Legacy

- 3,000+ killed, 40,000+ tortured under his rule
- Economic liberalization ("Chicago Boys")
- Never convicted of human rights crimes

---

## Manuel Contreras (1929-2015)

**Position**: DINA Director (1973-1977)
**Corpus mentions**: ~53 documents (combining variants)

### Background

- Army colonel
- Commanded Tejas Verdes detention facility
- Handpicked by Pinochet to create DINA

### Key Actions

- Built DINA into "Pinochet's Gestapo"
- Coordinated Operation Condor
- Ordered Letelier assassination
- Met CIA at Langley (March 1974)
- Briefly paid CIA asset (1975)

### Justice

- Convicted for Letelier murder (1993)
- Subsequently convicted for dozens of other crimes
- Died in prison (2015)

---

## Carlos Prats (1915-1974)

**Position**: Army Commander (1970-1973), Interior Minister
**Corpus mentions**: Part of corpus, exact count varies

### Background

- Constitutionalist military officer
- Supported elected government
- Army Commander under Allende

### Key Events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| August 23, 1973 | Resigned under military pressure |
| | Recommended Pinochet as successor |
| September 1973 | Exiled to Buenos Aires after coup |
| September 30, 1974 | **Assassinated** by DINA car bomb with wife Sofía |

### Significance

His assassination demonstrated DINA's international reach and willingness to kill former military leaders.

## Eduardo Frei Montalva (1911-1982)

**Position**: President (1964-1970)
**Party**: Christian Democrats (PDC)
**Corpus mentions**: ~45 documents (combining variants)

### Background

- Christian Democratic leader
- "Revolution in Liberty" reform program
- Received CIA support in 1964 election

### Role in Crisis

| Period | Position |
|--------|----------|
| 1964-1970 | President (preceded Allende) |
| 1970-1973 | Senator, increasingly opposed Allende |
| August 1973 | Supported declaration that Allende was unconstitutional |
| Post-coup | Initially supported military, later opposed |

### Death

Died 1982 under suspicious circumstances. 2009 investigation concluded he was poisoned, likely by dictatorship agents.

---

## Ronni Moffitt (1951-1976)

**Position**: Institute for Policy Studies colleague of Letelier
**Corpus mentions**: 37 documents (66 including variants)

### Background

- American citizen
- Worked at Institute for Policy Studies in Washington
- Colleague and friend of Letelier

### Death

- Killed alongside Letelier (September 21, 1976)
- Passenger in Letelier's car when bomb detonated
- Her death made case a US criminal matter (American victim)
- Husband Michael Moffitt survived (was also in car)

## René Schneider (1913-1970)

**Position**: Army Commander-in-Chief
**Significance**: Embodied constitutional military doctrine

### "Schneider Doctrine"

The principle that the military must respect the constitution and not intervene in politics, regardless of who wins elections.

### Assassination

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| October 22, 1970 | Killed in botched kidnapping attempt |
| | Plot aimed to create chaos, prevent Allende's confirmation |
| | CIA provided weapons to plotters (Track II) |

### Legacy

His assassination failed to prevent Allende's inauguration and created martyr for constitutionalism.

**Position**: MIR Secretary General (1967-1974)

### Background

- Medical doctor
- Led MIR from age 23
- Advocated armed revolution
- Did not join Popular Unity (too moderate)
- Provided personal security for Allende

### Death

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| October 5, 1974 | Killed by DINA in San Miguel, Santiago |
| | House surrounded by DINA agents |
| | Died covering retreat of pregnant wife Carmen Castillo |

## Bernardo Leighton (1909-1995)

**Position**: PDC leader, Vice President under Frei

### Background

- Christian Democratic co-founder
- Minister, Vice President
- Opposed both Allende AND the coup

### Assassination Attempt

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| October 6, 1975 | Shot in Rome by DINA-linked assassins |
| | Survived but severely wounded |
| | Wife Ana Fresno also shot, survived |

---

## Michael Townley (1942-)

**Position**: DINA operative (American citizen)

### Background

- American citizen who worked for DINA
- Electronics and explosives expert
- Recruited by DINA in early 1970s

### Crimes

- Built car bomb that killed Carlos Prats (1974)
- Built car bomb that killed Orlando Letelier (1976)
- Involved in Leighton assassination attempt

### Fate

- Turned state's witness
- Testified against DINA leadership
- Entered US witness protection program
- Served reduced sentence

**Corpus mentions**: 24 documents

## Pedro Espinoza (1933-)

### Role

- Contreras's deputy
- Directed DINA's operational activities
- Supervised international operations

### Justice

- Convicted for Letelier assassination
- Convicted for multiple other crimes
- Imprisoned

**Corpus mentions**: 17 documents

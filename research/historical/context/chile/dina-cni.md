# DINA and CNI: Chilean Secret Police

The intelligence agencies that conducted state terrorism under the Pinochet regime.

---

## DINA (Dirección de Inteligencia Nacional)

**Period**: 1973-1977
**Director**: Colonel Manuel Contreras
**Character**: Secret police, described as "Pinochet's Gestapo"

### Establishment

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| November 1973 | Created as Army intelligence unit |
| June 1974 | Decree 521 made DINA independent agency |

**Decree 521** contained three secret articles (9, 10, 11) that:
- Subordinated all other intelligence services to DINA
- Gave DINA unlimited power to raid houses
- Allowed arrests without charges

### Chain of Command

```
Augusto Pinochet
    ↓
Manuel Contreras (Director)
    ↓
Pedro Espinoza (Operations Chief)
    ↓
Brigade Commanders
    ↓
Arrest and Interrogation Teams
```

**Key point**: DINA reported directly to Pinochet, not to the junta.

### Organizational Structure

| Section | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Government Service** | Internal security |
| **Internal** | Domestic operations |
| **Economics** | Economic intelligence |
| **Psychological Warfare** | Propaganda |
| **External (Foreign Operations)** | International assassinations |

### Arrest Brigades

DINA's shock troops were the **Brigades of Arrests and Interrogation**:
- Teams of 5-6 persons under captain/major
- Named after Chilean indigenous groups (Antumapu, Pehuenche, Peldehue)
- Operated "security houses" for torture
- Wore civilian clothes

### Detention Centers

DINA operated at least **13 clandestine centers** in Santiago alone:

| Center | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Villa Grimaldi** (Cuartel Terranova) | Most notorious; ~4,500 detained, 226+ disappeared |
| **Londres 38** | Early detention center |
| **Venda Sexy** | Named for use of sexual torture |
| **Tejas Verdes** | Army base, early interrogation site |
| **Colonia Dignidad** | German cult compound used for torture |

### Methods

Documented torture methods included:
- Electric shocks
- Simulated executions
- Sexual assault
- Psychological torment
- "Submarino" (near-drowning)
- Hanging by limbs

**Statistics**: Valech Report verified **27,653 victims** of political imprisonment and torture.

## CIA Relationship with DINA

### Direct Contact

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| February 1974 | Pinochet asked CIA Deputy Director Vernon Walters for assistance |
| March 1974 | Walters hosted Contreras at CIA headquarters (Langley) |
| Mid-1975 | CIA briefly put Contreras on payroll as paid asset |

### Technical Assistance

DINA received:
- Technical manuals from CIA
- Training materials
- Organizational models (from KCIA, Brazil's SNI, Iran's SAVAK)

### US Knowledge

The extent of CIA knowledge of DINA operations remains partially classified, but documents show US awareness of DINA's methods.

## DINA International Operations

### Operation Condor Coordinator

DINA was the **primary coordinator** of Operation Condor (see `../cold-war/operation-condor.md`).

On November 25, 1975 (Pinochet's 60th birthday), Contreras convened intelligence chiefs from Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Paraguay, and Uruguay in Santiago to formally establish Operation Condor.

### International Assassinations

| Date | Target | Location | Notes |
|------|--------|----------|-------|
| Sept 30, 1974 | Carlos Prats | Buenos Aires | Car bomb, killed with wife |
| Oct 6, 1975 | Bernardo Leighton | Rome | Shot, survived (badly wounded) |
| Sept 21, 1976 | Orlando Letelier | Washington DC | Car bomb, killed with Ronni Moffitt |

### Key Operatives

| Name | Role |
|------|------|
| **Manuel Contreras** | Director |
| **Pedro Espinoza** | Operations chief |
| **Michael Townley** | American operative, bomb maker |
| **Armando Fernández Larios** | Operative (Letelier case) |

**Michael Townley** was an American who worked for DINA. He built the car bomb that killed Letelier and the bomb that killed Prats.

## DINA Targets

### Primary Targets

1. **MIR** (Movement of the Revolutionary Left)
   - Miguel Enríquez killed October 5, 1974
   - Leadership largely eliminated by 1975

2. **Socialist Party leaders**
   - Many killed or disappeared

3. **Communist Party members**
   - Systematic persecution

4. **Allende government officials**
   - Letelier assassination most prominent

### Results

By 1977, DINA had achieved its military goals:
- MIR leadership destroyed
- Main UP leaders killed, imprisoned, or exiled
- Organized opposition eliminated

---

## Dissolution of DINA

### Letelier Case Consequences

The September 1976 assassination of Orlando Letelier in Washington DC created massive international pressure:
- FBI investigation implicated DINA
- US demanded accountability
- International isolation for Chile

### End of DINA

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| August 1977 | DINA dissolved by Decree Law 1878 |
| August 1977 | CNI created as successor |
| 1977 | Contreras replaced by Odlanier Mena |

## CNI (Centro Nacional de Informaciones)

**Period**: 1977-1990
**Character**: Successor to DINA, continued repression

### Directors

| Name | Period |
|------|--------|
| Odlanier Mena | 1977-1980 |
| Humberto Gordon | 1980-1986 |
| Hugo Salas Wenzel | 1986-1990 |

### Differences from DINA

| Aspect | DINA | CNI |
|--------|------|-----|
| **Period** | 1973-1977 | 1977-1990 |
| **Intensity** | Maximum repression | Continued but reduced |
| **International** | Active assassinations | More limited |
| **Legal status** | Secret | Publicly acknowledged |
| **Judicial power** | None | Significant |

### CNI Operations

CNI continued:
- Surveillance of opposition
- "False confrontations" (staged shootouts to kill MIR members)
- Political intelligence
- Some assassinations

CNI was also implicated in:
- Bank robberies
- Drug trafficking
- Weapons trafficking

### Dissolution

CNI dissolved **February 22, 1990** by Law 18,943, shortly before democratic transition.

Many agents transferred to:
- Military intelligence units
- DIDN (Dirección de Inteligencia de la Defensa Nacional)

## Key Figures

### Manuel Contreras (1929-2015)

**Position**: DINA Director (1973-1977)

**Background**:
- Army colonel
- Commanded Tejas Verdes detention facility
- Known for ruthlessness

**Post-DINA**:
- Convicted for Letelier assassination (1993)
- Convicted for numerous other crimes
- Died in prison 2015

**Corpus mentions**: ~53 documents (combining variants)

### Pedro Espinoza (1933-)

**Position**: DINA Operations Chief

**Significance**: Directed operational activities including international assassinations.

**Corpus mentions**: 17 documents

### Michael Townley (1942-)

**Position**: DINA operative (American citizen)

**Significance**:
- Built car bombs for Prats and Letelier assassinations
- Key witness in US prosecution
- Entered US witness protection

**Corpus mentions**: 24 documents

### Armando Fernández Larios (1954-)

**Position**: DINA operative

**Significance**:
- Participated in Letelier operation
- Later defected, testified against DINA
- Extradited to Chile 2021

**Corpus mentions**: 16 documents
